The Standard of Care: A Teaching Perspective

Moving beyond familiarity to true clinical mastery through the lens of nursing education.

In the legal arena, "Standard of Care" is often treated as a binary—either a nurse met it, or they did not. However, as an Assistant Clinical Professor responsible for training the next generation of pediatric nurses, I view the standard through a more nuanced lens: **How is the standard taught, how is it practiced under pressure, and how is its effectiveness measured?**

"Because I teach students in didactic, clinical, and simulation settings, I'm not only familiar with the standards, I am beyond well-versed in the details of the processes, the steps in which we teach the standards to incoming nurses, and the means by which we evaluate their effectiveness."

The Simulation Edge

Modern nursing education relies heavily on clinical simulation—controlled environments where students are tested on their ability to make split-second clinical judgments. In these settings, we don't just look for "correctness"; we look for the **Nursing Process** in action: Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, and Evaluation.

When I review a legal case, I apply this same academic rigor. I am not just looking at a medical record for errors; I am analyzing whether the "Steps of the Process" were followed. This perspective allows me to explain to a jury not just *what* happened, but *why* a deviation occurred at a fundamental educational level.

Bridging the Gap

The gap between a textbook standard and a bedside reality is where most litigation lives. By maintaining an active role in both clinical faculty and frontline consulting, I provide legal teams with an expert who can bridge that gap with authoritative, academic clarity.
